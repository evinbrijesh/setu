Millions of citizens in India face a persistent barrier to accessing government services: not the absence of entitlements, but the complexity of navigating multilingual forms, unclear eligibility criteria, and document requirements designed around literacy and English fluency they may not have. This project, Setu, addresses that gap through a voice-first conversational agent that lets users interact entirely by speaking in their native language, such as Hindi or Tamil, without typing or reading a single form. Built on Sarvam AI's Saaras speech-to-text and Bulbul text-to-speech models, the agent conversationally identifies what a user needs, be it a caste certificate or a scheme like PM Kisan, and asks clarifying questions one at a time to collect the required information. Unlike typical chatbot interfaces, Setu is architected as a durable, resumable state machine backed by persistent storage, meaning a user who disconnects mid-conversation can call back later and the agent will recall exactly where the process was left off. Once all required fields are gathered, the system auto-generates a completed, submission-ready form as a downloadable document. By treating bureaucratic navigation as a stateful task-completion problem rather than a simple question-answering exercise, Setu demonstrates how voice AI can meaningfully lower the barrier between citizens and the services they are entitled to.
